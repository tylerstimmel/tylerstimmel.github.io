Dodger Stadium (Los Angeles,CA)

Dodger Stadium was built over six decades ago. Walter O’Malley was the Dodger president at the time and was trying to have a new stadium built for his Brooklyn ball club. However, a deal could not be reached and he decided to take the team out to California. There he built Dodger Stadium in Los Angeles. Dodger Stadium is the third oldest continually used stadium in Major League Baseball. It has been home to numerous Hall of Famers and World Champions since. Its rich history combined with its great location make it a must visit stadium for baseball fans everywhere.

Wrigley Field (Chicago,IL)

Wrigley Field first opened in 1914 under the name Weeghman Park. It was then taken over by the Cubs who played their first home game at the stadium on April 20, 1916. It was then named Cubs park until 1926 before it officially changed its name to Wrigley Field. It is the second oldest stadium in the MLB and has been home to many iconic moments in Major League Baseball history. It is most known for its ivy-covered brick outfield wall. Wrigley Field goes by the nickname “The Friendly Confines.” This is stadium is usually on every baseball fans bucket list and is a historical landmark in the heart of Chicago.

Yankee Stadium (New York,NY)

Yankee stadium is one of the most well known landmarks in the United State of America. This stadium was recently built in 2002. It is modern, clean, and located in the heart of the Bronx. It is one of the nicest stadiums in the American League and home to the historically pronoun New York Yankees. It has replicas from the old Yankee Stadium including seats and other memorabilia as well. Visiting Yankee Stadium is definitely a fun experience that every fan should enjoy.

Fenway Park (Boston,MA)

Fenway Park is one of America’s most beloved ball parks. Best known for its large green wall in left field, Fenway Park has housed many historical moments as well as Hall of Fame players. It was built in 1 year in 1912. Today, it is one of the most recognizable places in Boston and around the United States in general. Every Major League Baseball fan should watch a game at Fenway at some point in their life.

PNC Park (Pittsburgh, PA)

PNC Park is located on the north side of Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. It opened in 2001 so it is a relatively new stadium. It has hosted concerts, All Star Games, and other sporting events. It is widely considered one of the best ball parks in America. It’s views of the Pittsburgh Skyline combines with amazing architecture that offers a clear view from every seat. This park is a unique and mesmerizing landmark that every fan should see.
